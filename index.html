(BAMPA)

About Barangay Balaring

Barangay Balaring is a coastal barangay situated 5 kilometers northwest of Silay City, Negros Occidental.

The coastal portion of the area has a hydrosol type of soil, suited to mangrove tree species, nipa, palms, and salt beds.

Fishing is the major source of livelihood for communities living in the coastal barangays.

In the 1970’s, residents of Brgy. Balaring witnessed the massive cutting of the mangroves in their place. Locally-known species such as Bungalon, Alipata and Bakauan were cut down for fuel and charcoal either for commercial or personal consumption. Fishpond were also expanded because of the rising demand for prawn in local and international markets. Small fishermen started to complain of the diminishing fish catch and sea shells despite the fact that Balaring waters is a fishing ground of Silay City. This problem greatly affected the people’s source of income, and more so, their environment.

Recognizing the ill-effects of the diminishing mangrove cover, initially 35 heads of families decided to organize the Balaring Mangrove Planters Association (BAMPA) in 1991 with the objective of rehabilitating mangroves, thus improving the breeding ground of the fish species, shells and crabs.

BAMPA- a self-help organization which has sustained a mangrove enterprise and institutionalized the full participation of the fisherfolks in the decision-making, planning, implementation and monitoring of mangrove plantation projects.

Presently, more than 50 hectares of mangroves were established and maintained
